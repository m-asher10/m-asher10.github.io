About me

Hello! I'm a University of Pittsburgh alum with a passion for linguistics, specifically sociophonetics, computational linguistics, and, relatedly, gender studies. At Pitt, I earned a Bachelor of Philosophy (BPhil) degree in lingustics by conducting research focused on the perception of diverse vocal genders in speech. I'm especially interested in taking an individual differences approach to research, focusing on how an individual's own experiences impact their perceptions and behaviors.

Outside of my own research, I've been an assistant and trainer in a few other linguistics labs at Pitt. When I'm not staring at my computer, I can be found crocheting, jamming to DJ sets, going on walks with friends, and adoring my dog, Bella.

Research

Some of my recent research endeavors...

Defended my thesis (and passed!)

To earn my BPhil, I conducted funded research exploring individual differences in the perception of diverse vocal genders. My committee consisted of Drs. Melinda Fricke, Dan Villarreal, Scott Kiesling, and Keith Johnson (UC Berkeley).

Presented at NCUR 2025

In April, I presented my BPhil research at the National Conference of Undergraduate Research (NCUR) in Pittsburgh.

Fricke Language Lab

I assisted in a few different projects in Dr. Fricke's lab, including eye-tracking, phonetic memory, and sociophonetic studies.

APLS Lab

Under Dr. Villarreal, I transcribed sociolinguistic interviews for the Archive of Pittsburgh Language and Speech (APLS) and trained new RAs. Pictured here is our lab picnic at Frick Park!
